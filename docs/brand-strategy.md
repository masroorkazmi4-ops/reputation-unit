# Reputation Unit — Brand Strategy

## 1. Brand Positioning

Reputation Unit is a partner-led web development studio that builds modern websites, web apps, AI tools, and business systems for small businesses and startups.

It is not a large agency. It is not a solo freelancer. It is a focused two-partner studio that delivers serious, production-quality digital work — the kind of work that clients can stake their reputation on.

The name *Reputation Unit* reflects this: every project we build becomes a unit of our reputation. We only ship work we'd put our name on.

---

## 2. One-Line Positioning Statement

> Partner-led web development studio building modern websites, AI tools, and business systems that small businesses can stake their reputation on.

Short variant for hero:
> We build the websites and systems your business runs on.

---

## 3. Homepage Hero Message Direction

The hero must accomplish three things in under five seconds:

1. **Communicate what Reputation Unit does.** Websites, web apps, AI tools, business systems.
2. **Communicate who it is for.** Small businesses, startups, service companies, local businesses.
3. **Establish credibility without faking it.** Studio-grade quality, real client work, partner-led accountability.

**Recommended headline:**
"We build websites and digital systems that businesses rely on."

**Recommended subheadline:**
"Partner-led studio. Modern websites, web apps, AI tools, dashboards, and business systems — built for clarity, speed, and conversion."

The hero should feel authoritative, not desperate. No "Welcome to our website." No "Your digital partner." No overused startup copy.

## 10. Trust Strategy Without Fake Proof

Since there are no testimonials, no awards, and no 100-client portfolio yet, trust must be built through:

1. **Real portfolio work.** Show MK Digitizing as a live public case study. Present Food Service App and Law Firm Website honestly as private/launch-ready builds.
2. **Quality of the website itself.** This site IS the portfolio. If it looks premium, fast, and polished, it proves the studio's skill.
3. **Transparent process.** Show exactly how a project works: Discover → Structure → Design → Build → Review → Launch → Improve.
4. **Honest language.** "Selected client work" instead of "Hundreds of satisfied clients." "Partner-led studio" instead of "Global agency."
5. **Specific capabilities.** List actual tech stack and tools used — not vague buzzwords.
6. **Responsive, well-designed contact form.** The form itself demonstrates care and professionalism.
7. **Clear scope explanation.** Pricing transparency through quote-based language, not hidden fees.

## 11. Portfolio Presentation Strategy

### MK Digitizing (Public)
- Show as a full case study card
- Include link to mkdigitizing.com
- Describe the industry, the build, and the tech used
- Include 2-3 highlight points about what was built

### Food Service App (Private)
- Show as a portfolio card with an honest privacy note
- "Private business system — preview available on request"
- Describe the type of system built (food ordering, service management)
- Do not invent a public URL

### Law Firm Website (Private/Launch-Ready)
- Show as a portfolio card with an honest status note
- "Launch-ready build — private preview available until final domain is connected"
- Describe the type of site and industry
- Do not invent a public URL

## 12. Contact / Conversion Strategy

- Primary CTA: "Start a Project" → leads to a project inquiry form
- Secondary CTA: "View Work" → scrolls to portfolio section
- The contact form is the main conversion mechanism
- No chat widget, no phone number, no calendar booking — keep it clean
- Form should collect: name, business name, email, project type, optional budget range, optional timeline, message
- The form should feel premium — not a basic HTML form

---

## 13. Pricing Communication Strategy

- Do not show fixed pricing tiers
- Use quote-based positioning: "Every project is quoted based on scope, features, timeline, and business requirements."
- Explain what affects cost: number of pages, design complexity, forms, dashboards, AI features, integrations, maintenance
- The FAQ section should address pricing questions honestly
- If a budget range field is included in the form, make it optional and non-committal

## 15. Recommended Homepage Narrative Flow

The homepage should tell this story in order:

1. **Hero** — What Reputation Unit does, who it's for, and why it's different. Two CTAs: View Work + Start a Project.
2. **Social proof strip** — Not fake logos. Instead: "3 client projects delivered" or "Public & private builds" — factual, low-key.
3. **Services** — What we build. Cards or grid. Clear, scannable.
4. **Selected Work / Portfolio** — The three real projects with honest labels.
5. **Process** — How a project works. Linear flow: Discover → Structure → Design → Build → Review → Launch → Improve.
6. **Capabilities / Tech Stack** — What we use. Tech logos or labels in a grid.
7. **Why Reputation Unit** — 3-4 differentiators. Partner-led. Business-focused. Modern stack. Real systems.
8. **FAQ** — Address cost, timeline, scope, hosting, domains, revisions, AI tools.
9. **Contact / CTA** — Project inquiry form. Strong closing headline. Final CTA.
10. **Footer** — Reputation Unit wordmark, nav links, contact email placeholder, copyright.

This order moves from attention → credibility → proof → process → trust → conversion.
